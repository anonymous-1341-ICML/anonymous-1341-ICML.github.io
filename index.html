Abstract

TL;DR

The Forward-Forward (FF) algorithm has emerged as a biologically plausible alternative to backpropagation, yet it suffers from limited representational capacity due to reliance on local signals. We first analyze why forward learning performs much worse than backpropagation and categorize the issues into two types of errors: Information Error (activity difference vanishes at decision boundaries) and Approximation Error (gradient estimation instability).

We propose Tri-Stream Coupled Dynamics (TSCD), featuring: (1) A tri-stream framework with cross-fusion to mine latent discriminative features; (2) MP-GBS and TF-GVS optimization strategies to suppress gradient bias and variance. TSCD achieves 95.78% on CIFAR-10 (vs. 96.45% BP), effectively narrowing the gap while maintaining biological plausibility.

Approximation Error

The forward gradient is fundamentally a zero-order estimator. Variance is amplified by the inverse square of the nudging factor, while Bias emerges from curvature-induced drift in the Hessian.

$$\text{Var}(\hat{g}) \propto \frac{1}{\gamma^2}, \quad \mathbb{E}[\hat{g}] = g_{BP} + \frac{\gamma}{2}h^\top H h$$

State Update Rules

$$\mathbf{u}_l \leftarrow f_l\left(W_{l-1}\bar{h}_{l-1} + \frac{\lambda\gamma_l}{\gamma_{l+1}}W_l^\top(\mathbf{u}_{l+1} - \mathbf{v}_{l+1})\right)$$ $$\mathbf{v}_l \leftarrow f_l\left(W_{l-1}\bar{h}_{l-1} - \frac{(1-\lambda)\gamma_l}{\gamma_{l+1}}W_l^\top(\mathbf{u}_{l+1} - \mathbf{v}_{l+1})\right)$$

Key Theoretical Result

The normalized state difference exactly recovers BP gradients in the weak nudging limit (Œ≥ ‚Üí 0‚Å∫):

$$\lim_{\gamma_l \to 0^+} \frac{\mathbf{u}_l - \mathbf{v}_l}{\gamma_l} = f'_l(\mathbf{a}_l) \cdot \delta_{l+1} \propto \nabla_{BP}$$
Bias Suppression

MP-GBS: Multi-Plane Forward Gradient Bias Suppression

Aggregates gradients from multiple random perturbation directions using conjugate norm pairs {(pk, qk)} to find a consensus direction d* that aligns closer to the true gradient.

$$d^* = \arg\max_{d \in \bigcap_{j=1}^{3}\Pi_j} \langle d, \nabla_D\mathcal{L}(W) \rangle$$

Improves gradient alignment by 35% (Figure 1c)

Variance Suppression

TF-GVS: Training Free Gradient Variance Suppression

Averages activation maps across M=3 continuous temporal steps within a batch before gradient computation, treating consecutive batches as samples from a locally stationary curvature cluster.

$$\bar{\nabla}_D\mathcal{L}_l = \frac{1}{M}\sum_{i=1}^{M}\hat{G}_l^{(i)}, \quad \text{Var}(\bar{\nabla}_D\mathcal{L}) = \frac{\sigma^2}{M}$$

Reduces variance by 70% (Figure 1b)
