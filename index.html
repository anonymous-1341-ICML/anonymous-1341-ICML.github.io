Abstract

Standard Forward Forward (FF) learning optimizes layer-wise objectives without backward error propagation but suffers from gradient locality, negative sample dependency, and high variance. In this paper, we introduce TS-CDN, a novel architecture that addresses these limitations. The key insight is Dyadic Neuron Dynamics: maintaining two internal states per neuron (excited and relaxed) to encode gradient information in their difference. This eliminates the need for explicit backward computation while provably recovering Backpropagation (BP) gradients in the weak nudging limit. Furthermore, we propose a Tri-Stream Architecture (Positive, Negative, and Cross-Fusion streams) to explicitly mine boundary features. Combined with Multi-Plane Forward Dyadic Sharpness (MP-FDS) and Zero-Order Energy Variance Suppression (ZEVS), our method achieves stable and robust optimization.


Methodology

1. Dyadic Neuron Dynamics

A dyadic neuron at layer \( l \) maintains two states: an excited state \( \mathbf{u}_l \) and a relaxed state \( \mathbf{v}_l \). The states evolve according to:

$$ \mathbf{u}_l = f_l \left( \mathbf{W}_{l-1}\bar{\mathbf{h}}_{l-1} + \lambda \gamma_l \boldsymbol{\delta}_{l+1} \right) $$ $$ \mathbf{v}_l = f_l \left( \mathbf{W}_{l-1}\bar{\mathbf{h}}_{l-1} - (1 - \lambda) \gamma_l \boldsymbol{\delta}_{l+1} \right) $$

where \( \gamma_l \) is the nudging factor. Their difference encodes how the layer should change to improve performance. Remarkably, in the weak nudging limit (\( \gamma_l \to 0^+ \)), the normalized dyadic difference exactly recovers the backpropagation gradient:

$$ \lim_{\gamma_l \to 0^+} \frac{\mathbf{u}_l - \mathbf{v}_l}{\gamma_l} = f'_l(\mathbf{a}_l) \odot \left( \mathbf{W}_l^\top \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \mathbf{h}_{l+1}} \right) $$


2. Tri-Stream Architecture with Cross-Fusion


Standard FF misses discriminative features at class boundaries. TS-CDN employs three parallel streams:

  • Positive Stream (\(\mathcal{N}_P\)): Optimizes for high goodness on positive samples.
  • Negative Stream (\(\mathcal{N}_N\)): Optimizes for low goodness on negative samples.
  • Cross-Fusion Stream (\(\mathcal{N}_C\)): Periodically "transplants" states from the converged parallel streams to create a "chimeric" network. The difference \( \mathbf{u}^C_l - \mathbf{v}^C_l \) captures the discriminative boundary between classes.


Optimization Strategies

MP-FDS

Multi-Plane Forward Dyadic Sharpness improves generalization by seeking flat minima. Unlike standard SAM, MP-FDS explores multiple geometric views simultaneously using multi-norm perturbations to find a robust consensus direction \( \mathbf{d}^* \).

ZEVS

Zero-Order Energy Variance Suppression addresses the high variance of zero-order gradient estimators. It uses temporal averaging over a sliding window of \( M=3 \) batches to stabilize optimization without significant computational overhead.

Biological Plausibility: TS-CDN adheres to biological constraints by avoiding weight transport and global error signals. The tri-stream architecture can be interpreted as separate neural populations for positive, negative, and integration processing.
