Abstract

The Forward-Forward (FF) algorithm has emerged as a biologically plausible alternative to backpropagation, yet it suffers from limited representational capacity due to reliance on local signals. In this work, we propose the Tri-Stream Coupled Dyadic Network (TS-CDN), a novel forward learning framework designed to solve two limitations: Information Error and Approximation Error. TS-CDN introduces three key innovations: (1) Dyadic neurons that theoretically recover exact BP gradients in the weak-nudging limit; (2) A tri-stream architecture with a cross-fusion mechanism that mitigates Information Error by mining latent discriminative features from parallel streams; and (3) Two complementary optimization strategies, Multi Plane Forward Gradient Bias Suppression (MP-GBS) and Training Free Gradient Variance Suppression (TF-GVS), that counteract Approximation Error by minimizing estimation bias and variance, respectively. Notably, TS-CDN achieves 96.12% on CIFAR-10 with ConvNeXt-Tiny and 98.92% average accuracy on BreakHis with EfficientNetV2-S, significantly narrowing the gap with backpropagation while maintaining full biological plausibility.


Methodology

1. Dyadic Neuron Dynamics

Unlike traditional neurons, each neuron in TS-CDN maintains two intrinsic states: an excited state \(\mathbf{u}\) (positive activation) and a relaxed state \(\mathbf{v}\) (negative activation). The inference is defined via a closed-form relaxation:

$$ \mathbf{u}_l \leftarrow f_l \left( \mathbf{W}_{l-1}\bar{\mathbf{h}}_{l-1} + \frac{\lambda\gamma_l}{\gamma_{l+1}} \mathbf{W}_l^\top (\mathbf{u}_{l+1} - \mathbf{v}_{l+1}) \right) $$ $$ \mathbf{v}_l \leftarrow f_l \left( \mathbf{W}_{l-1}\bar{\mathbf{h}}_{l-1} - \frac{(1-\lambda)\gamma_l}{\gamma_{l+1}} \mathbf{W}_l^\top (\mathbf{u}_{l+1} - \mathbf{v}_{l+1}) \right) $$

Crucially, we prove that the normalized state difference exactly recovers the backpropagation gradient in the weak nudging limit (\(\gamma_l \to 0^+\)): $$ \lim_{\gamma_l \to 0^+} \frac{\mathbf{u}_l - \mathbf{v}_l}{\gamma_l} = f'_l(\mathbf{a}_l) \cdot \delta_{l+1} \propto \text{BP Gradient} $$


2. Tri-Stream Architecture & Cross-Fusion

To mitigate Information Error caused by vanishing signals in local updates, we introduce a Cross-Fusion Stream \(\mathcal{N}_C\). Periodically, states are transplanted from the Positive (\(\mathcal{N}_P\)) and Negative (\(\mathcal{N}_N\)) streams to \(\mathcal{N}_C\). The cross-error signal \(\Delta W^C\) minimizes manifold misalignment, bridging the gap between local and global optimization.


3. Robust Optimization Strategies

MP-GBS (Bias Suppression)
Multi Plane Forward Gradient Bias Suppression. Standard gradients in forward learning suffer from estimation bias. MP-GBS aggregates gradients from multiple random perturbation directions (intersection of multiple planes) to find a consensus direction \(\mathbf{d}^*\) that aligns closer to the true gradient.
TF-GVS (Variance Suppression)
Training Free Gradient Variance Suppression. To counteract high variance in local estimation, TF-GVS averages activation maps across continuous temporal steps within a batch before gradient computation, theoretically reducing variance by factor \(M\).
